# Items excluded from the catalog, and why

Not legal advice — flagging these so you and your attorney can make the final call
on the complete list. This isn't exhaustive; it covers the items with the clearest
regulatory red flags from the original price list.

## Excluded

**FDA-approved prescription drugs (require a prescription from a licensed pharmacy for human use):**
- Semaglutide (Ozempic/Wegovy)
- Tirzepatide (Mounjaro/Zepbound)
- Retatrutide, Cagrilintide, Mazdutide, Survodutide — investigational/approved-abroad GLP-1-class drugs marketed the same way
- PT-141 / Bremelanotide (Vyleesi)
- Tesamorelin (Egrifta)
- HCG (prescription-only)

**Specifically flagged by FDA / known safety concerns:**
- BPC-157 and all BPC-157 blends (BB, BBG70, Klow) — removed from FDA's 503A compounding bulk substances list in 2023
- TB-500 / Thymosin Beta-4 — same 503A exclusion
- Melanotan I/II — no approved human use, documented adverse effects

## Borderline — worth a specific legal opinion

- **Sermorelin** — has prior FDA-approval history (as Geref, discontinued), commonly sold industry-wide as a research peptide today.
- **IGF-1 LR3, Oxytocin, HGH Frag 176-191** — widely sold in the research-peptide space, but have known off-label human-use markets (bodybuilding, wellness clinics) worth flagging to counsel.

## Everything else in the catalog

The remaining ~70 items (Epithalon, Selank, Semax, CJC-1295 variants, Ipamorelin,
GHK-Cu, Thymosin Alpha-1, NAD+, and others) are commonly sold by research-chemical
suppliers today. That's a market reality, not a legal guarantee — have your attorney
review the final list against current FDA guidance before launch, since this changes
periodically (the BPC-157/TB-500 exclusion above is a recent example).
