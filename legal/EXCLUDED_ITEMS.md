# Items previously excluded from the catalog

Not legal advice — flagging these so you and your attorney can make the final call.
This isn't exhaustive; it covers the items with the clearest regulatory red flags
from the original price list.

## Decision: included despite the flags below

On 2026-07-12, after this risk was explained, the business decision was made to
include all items below in the live catalog anyway, on the theory that a
research-use-only (RUO) label and buyer certification means these restrictions
don't apply. Recording that reasoning here for the record, along with the
counter-argument, since a future attorney review should weigh both:

**Why "RUO label" may not be sufficient on its own:** FDA and courts evaluate
intended use from the *totality of the circumstances*, not the label alone —
including whether a product is chemically identical to an approved drug, whether
it's packaged/dosed the way people self-administer it, and whether there's a
known consumer market buying that exact product from that kind of storefront for
personal use. The FDA has issued warning letters to other "research peptide"
sellers carrying an RUO label on products in the first category below. For the
second category (BPC-157/TB-500), the exclusion isn't about marketing at all —
FDA removed them from the 503A bulk substances list based on their own safety
review, independent of how the seller labels the product.
